## THE STABLE RUNTIME PRINCIPLE

DreamTalez stories are **always** 8–10 minutes read aloud. This is the bedtime ritual contract.

Children calibrate sleep onset to ritual length. A story that ends too soon disrupts the signal. A story that runs too long causes fatigue-resistance.

**Target: 1,200–1,500 words.**
**Minimum: 1,200 words.**
**Maximum: 1,500 words.**

What adapts within this container: emotional weight, pacing distribution, sensory intensity, prose cadence. Never the total runtime.

## SENTENCE PACING BY SECTION

### Opening (Stage 1)
- Average sentence: 14–18 words
- Rhythm: flowing, with internal pauses
- Short sentences: 2–3 per section, for emotional punctuation

### Adventure (Stage 2)
- Average sentence: 12–16 words
- Rhythm: forward momentum, varied
- Short sentences: 3–4, landing wonder beats

### Middle (Stage 3)
- Average sentence: 10–14 words
- Rhythm: slowing, settling
- Short sentences: increasing frequency

### Heart Moment (Stage 4)
- Average sentence: 8–12 words
- Rhythm: deliberate pauses
- Short sentences: dominant

### Ending (Stage 5)
- Average sentence: 6–10 words
- Rhythm: very slow — almost still
- Final sentence: 3–8 words, complete

## PARAGRAPH COUNT TARGETS

- Total paragraphs: 10–20 for a standard story
- Opening: 2–3 paragraphs
- Adventure: 4–6 paragraphs
- Middle: 3–5 paragraphs
- Heart Moment: 2–3 paragraphs
- Ending: 3–4 paragraphs (+ 2–3 for late bedtime)

---

## FORBIDDEN LENGTH VIOLATIONS

**Too short (under 1,200 words):**
The ritual feels incomplete. The child doesn't reach sleep-readiness before the story ends. This is the most damaging error.

**Too long (over 1,500 words):**
The child may become restless or the parent loses the rhythm. Fatigue-resistance increases.

**Unbalanced sections:**
- An ending under 150 words for a late-bedtime child fails the deceleration arc
- An adventure over 600 words overwhelms the emotional arc
- A heart moment under 100 words prevents genuine emotional landing
