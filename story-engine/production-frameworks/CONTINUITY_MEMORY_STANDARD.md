# CONTINUITY MEMORY STANDARD
## Bedtalez Story & Character Consistency System
### Authored by Claude Opus | Locked for Sonnet Runtime

---

## CONTINUITY AS TRUST

A child who hears their name pronounced differently mid-story loses trust. A child who hears a character change personality loses immersion. A child who hears a world that contradicts itself loses the magic.

Continuity is not a technical requirement. It is the foundation of the emotional contract.

---

## CHARACTER CONTINUITY RULES

### The Child Hero
- Name: used exactly as provided, consistent spelling and form throughout
- Pronoun: consistent with profile — never changes mid-story
- Personality: consistent flaw and strength from introduction to resolution
- Age-appropriate language: vocabulary and sentence complexity matched to age throughout

### Supporting Characters
- Name: used consistently every time they appear
- Personality: the same character must sound and behave the same way across appearances
- Role: their function in the story does not shift unexpectedly
- Voice: their dialogue has a consistent register (a grandmother sounds like a grandmother throughout)

### The World
- Setting: one primary setting per story with logically connected sub-locations
- Rules of the world: if magic works one way, it works that way consistently
- Timeline: events follow causally — no time jumps, no unexplained gaps
- Objects: objects introduced early may reappear; objects cannot disappear unexplained

---

## GOAL CONTINUITY

Every story has one central WANT (external goal) and one NEED (internal discovery). Both must:

1. Be established in Stage 1 or Stage 2
2. Remain present throughout the middle sections
3. Be resolved explicitly and on-page
4. Not be abandoned, replaced, or forgotten

The child must complete what they set out to do. An unresolved WANT at the end is a broken promise.

---

## FAMILY MAGIC CONTINUITY

When Family Magic data is present:

- Comfort items introduced must appear at logical moments, not scattered randomly
- Family members named in the profile must appear with the exact names and relationships provided
- The magical place (if provided) should shape the story world or appear naturally
- The cozy feeling (if provided) should be reflected in the atmosphere

The child's profile is a contract. The story must honor it.

---

## WORLD GROUNDING

The setting must feel physically real throughout the story. Rules:

- Reinforce the setting with subtle sensory details every 3–4 paragraphs (not constant, but regular)
- Never let the reader wonder "where are we now?" without a clear answer in the next paragraph
- Transitions between locations must carry an atmospheric thread (see Cinematic Prose Standard)
- The setting at the end should feel connected to the setting at the start — same world, same emotional temperature

---

## LANGUAGE CONTINUITY

If the story is in a non-English language:
- No English words may appear
- Grammar and phrasing must be natural for that language — not translated English
- Dialect consistency: if British English, no American spellings; if American, no British

If a dialect is specified:
- Apply consistently from first word to last
- Correct any drift in the QA pass

---

## SERIES CONTINUITY

When series context is provided (a recurring world, companion, or character arc):

- The established world rules apply — do not reinvent them
- The recurring companion has the same name, personality, and role
- The emotional thread from the previous story is acknowledged (subtly, not forced)
- New characters and events must fit the established world

The child's personal story universe must feel like it grows — not like it resets.

---

## CONSISTENCY CHECK PROTOCOL

Before delivering any story, verify:
- [ ] Child's name consistent throughout
- [ ] Child's pronoun consistent throughout  
- [ ] Setting is the same place at start and end (unless transition was explicitly written)
- [ ] WANT is resolved on-page
- [ ] NEED is resolved at the Heart Moment
- [ ] No character's personality contradicts their introduction
- [ ] Language/dialect is consistent throughout
- [ ] Comfort anchors appear at most twice and feel natural

---

*Consistency is the invisible architecture. When it fails, the child falls out of the world. When it holds, the world holds them.*
