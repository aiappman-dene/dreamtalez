# FAMILY MAGIC CONSTITUTION
## Bedtalez Family Warmth System
### Authored by Claude Opus | Locked for Sonnet Runtime

---

## CORE LAW

**The child is always the hero. Without exception. Without compromise.**

Family members in Family Magic stories exist to love the child — not to solve, rescue, or lead. Their presence is warmth. Their function is emotional safety. The child's courage is the engine of every adventure.

---

## FAMILY MAGIC PHILOSOPHY

Family Magic stories are not adventure stories with family in them. They are love stories told through adventure. The family's presence — even when they are not in the scene — is the invisible warmth that makes the child's courage possible.

The goal: when the child hears their real family member's name in the story, they feel seen, loved, and safe. The story becomes a mirror of their real world, made magical.

---

## FAMILY MEMBER RULES

### Appearance
- Each family member appears at most **once** per story
- Appearances are brief, warm, and purposeful
- They appear at emotionally appropriate moments — never randomly

### Role
- Family members **encourage** — they never instruct or command
- Family members **celebrate** the child's success — they do not create it
- Family members **provide warmth** — they do not provide solutions
- Family members speak in short, loving lines — not speeches

### What family members NEVER do
- Solve the central challenge
- Lead the adventure
- Rescue the child from danger
- Overshadow the child's emotional arc
- Give the child the answer they need

### Dialogue examples
**Wrong:** "Here, let me help you cross the bridge — I know the way."
**Right:** "You've got this," Mum said softly, and then she was gone, and Emma was brave.

---

## COMFORT ITEM RULES

### Placement
- Referenced at most **twice** per story
- First appearance: during a moment of uncertainty or challenge
- Second appearance (optional): in the closing scene, providing emotional resolution

### Tone
- Comfort items must feel **discovered**, not placed
- They appear naturally, as if they were always there
- They connect the magical world to the child's real world

### Example
**Wrong:** "She remembered to bring her teddy bear, which helped her feel better."
**Right:** "And there it was — her favourite blanket, folded over a root at the edge of the clearing, as if the forest had known she might need it."

---

## SCENE WARMTH GUIDE

| Story Section | Family Presence | Guidance |
|---------------|----------------|---------|
| Opening | Present, brief | A warm goodbye gesture before the adventure begins |
| Adventure | Absent from scenes | Family love travels inside the child — not in the scenes |
| Emotional Moment | Memory only | Child draws on remembered warmth — comfort item or recalled moment |
| Ending | Brief return | One short, loving exchange; the world settles |

---

## EMOTIONAL CONTINUITY SYSTEM

Family Magic stories carry **cozy callbacks** — small echoes of the child's real world woven naturally into the magical world:

- A comfort item that appears at the right moment
- A phrase the family member might say, remembered mid-adventure
- A sensory detail (warmth, smell, texture) associated with home
- A magical place shaped by what makes bedtime feel safe for this child

These callbacks are not decoration. They are the emotional architecture of the story. They tell the child: *your real world is here too, and it loves you.*

---

## ENDING REQUIREMENT

Family Magic stories must end with:
1. The child safe, warm, and proud
2. At least one comfort anchor (comfort item OR family warmth moment)
3. The night world settling softly — stars, moonlight, quiet
4. A final line that carries the feeling: *I am loved, and I am brave, and I am home*

---

## WHAT FAMILY MAGIC MUST NEVER DO

- Make a family member more heroic than the child
- Create conflict between the child and family members
- Use family magic as a crutch when the story lacks an adventure
- Reference family members in every scene (subtlety = depth)
- Make comfort items feel like plot devices rather than emotional anchors

---

*Family Magic is not a feature. It is a philosophy. The child must always feel: this story was made for me, and the people I love are in it.*
