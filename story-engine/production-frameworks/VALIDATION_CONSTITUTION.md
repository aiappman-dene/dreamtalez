# VALIDATION CONSTITUTION
## Bedtalez Story Quality Gates
### Authored by Claude Opus | Locked for Sonnet Runtime

---

## VALIDATION PHILOSOPHY

Validation is not a checklist. It is a quality promise to the child and the parent.

Every story delivered by Bedtalez must pass the standard that a senior Disney editor would apply before publication. The validator's job is not to find faults — it is to confirm that this story deserves to be the last thing a child hears before sleep.

---

## MANDATORY QUALITY GATES

A story must pass ALL of the following before delivery:

### Gate 1: Emotional Warmth
- At minimum 4 distinct emotional warmth signals present (safe, loved, brave, warm, gentle, magical, wonder, home, belonging, etc.)
- Heart Moment is present and emotionally complete
- No emotional ambiguity at the end

### Gate 2: Bedtime Safety
- No exclamation marks in the final 30% of the story
- No "suddenly" in the final quarter
- No unresolved tension at the end
- Ending contains minimum 3 sleep-readiness signals
- No cliffhangers, no invitations to stay awake

### Gate 3: Story Integrity
- WANT (external goal) is established and resolved on-page
- NEED (internal realisation) is present at the Heart Moment
- Child is the hero — no family member or companion solves the challenge
- No generic opener ("Once upon a time", "There was a child named")
- No abrupt cut-off (story must end with terminal punctuation)

### Gate 4: Length
- Minimum: 1,200 words (or age-appropriate minimum for young children)
- Maximum: 1,500 words (or age-appropriate maximum)
- Not severely unbalanced across sections

### Gate 5: Language Consistency
- Dialect/language consistent throughout
- No English leak in non-English stories
- Character names and pronouns consistent

---

## WARNING-LEVEL ISSUES (LOG, DO NOT BLOCK)

These issues are flagged for quality improvement but do not prevent delivery:

- Sensory density above 40% of paragraphs
- Repetition of comfort words (softly/quietly/gently/glowing used 4+ times)
- Consecutive sensory paragraphs (3+)
- Final sentence longer than average sentence length
- Child's name appears fewer than 3 times
- Complex vocabulary for toddler/preschool age group

---

## FAMILY MAGIC VALIDATION

When Family Magic is active, additionally check:

- [ ] Child is the hero — family member did not solve the challenge
- [ ] Family members appear at most once each
- [ ] Comfort anchor appears at most twice
- [ ] Comfort anchor feels discovered, not placed
- [ ] Ending references comfort item or family warmth
- [ ] Story ends with child safe, warm, loved

---

## LANGUAGE VALIDATION

For non-English stories, additionally check:

- [ ] No English words leaked into the story
- [ ] Language-specific bedtime comfort words present in final section
- [ ] Story length not significantly shorter than English equivalent (translation truncation check)

---

## CINEMATIC FLOW VALIDATION

- [ ] No abrupt scene transitions ("Then they went to...")
- [ ] Comfort anchor (warmth, light, familiar object) returned in ending
- [ ] No new characters introduced after the Heart Moment
- [ ] Breathing space present between Heart Moment and Ending

---

## VALIDATION OUTCOME

**PASS** — deliver story as-is
**WARN** — deliver story, log warnings for quality monitoring
**FAIL** — trigger refinement pass or regeneration

A FAIL is triggered by:
- Any Gate 1–5 failure above
- An incomplete ending (cut-off, abrupt stop)
- Language mixing in non-English stories

---

*Validation is the guardian of the bedtime promise. Every story that passes is one a child can trust.*
