# Scene Structure Framework

## Purpose

Provides a reusable blueprint for scene construction across all stories.
Ensures consistency, pacing, and emotional impact.

## Scene Anatomy

### Opening (Establishment)
- Anchor the child in the world
- Introduce immediate sensory details
- Establish emotional tone
- Present the question or challenge

### Middle (Development)
- Deepen the child's engagement
- Build stakes and meaning
- Introduce secondary characters or elements
- Maintain sensory immersion

### Closing (Transition or Resolution)
- Resolve the scene's central tension
- Create a bridge to the next scene or conclusion
- Return the child to a place of safety/wonder
- Plant a question or curiosity for continuation

## Pacing Markers

- **Calm**: Gentle, soothing, grounding
- **Building**: Increasing engagement, curiosity
- **Peak**: Maximum emotional engagement (brief, controlled)
- **Resolution**: Tension releases into clarity
- **Rest**: Return to calm, preparation for next scene or sleep

## Continuity Rules

1. **Character Consistency**: No unexplained behavior changes
2. **Location Logic**: Transitions must make narrative sense
3. **Time Flow**: Clear passage of time or timelessness
4. **Emotional Coherence**: Feelings build on previous scenes

## Sensory Layering

Each scene should include:
- **Visual**: Imagery that transports
- **Auditory**: Sounds that create atmosphere
- **Tactile**: Textures and physical sensations
- **Olfactory**: Scents that ground the reader (used sparingly)
