# Family Magic Runtime Rules

## Core law

The child is ALWAYS the hero.

---

## Family warmth tone

Family warmth should feel:

- soft and ambient, like a light left on in a warm room
- emotionally grounding — the child knows love is there
- comforting without being present in every scene
- lightly woven in at the opening, emotional peak, and ending

---

## Scene-stage warmth guide

| Stage           | Family presence |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Opening         | Medium — a brief warm goodbye before the adventure |
| Adventure       | Low — family love travels inside the child; they are not there |
| Emotional moment | Medium — the child draws on remembered warmth |
| Ending          | Medium-high — a gentle homecoming, one tender exchange |

---

## Recurring comfort continuity

Use the child's comfort items as recurring motifs — not props, but emotional anchors:

- Mention naturally, not mechanically
- Present at the emotional peak as a grounding element
- Referenced softly in the closing
- Never repeated more than twice in the same story

### Default comfort atmosphere (if no items specified)

- warm blankets
- soft scarves
- glowing lanterns
- sleepy stars
- gentle fireplace light
- rain on windows

---

## Family reference rules

- Each family member may appear **once** per story
- One line or gesture — loving and brief
- They respond to the child's success; they do not cause it
- Dialogue: short, warm, entirely in their voice

---

## Atmosphere baseline

Every Family Magic story should carry:

- slow pacing that decelerates toward the ending
- soft sensory textures (warmth, glow, rain, starlight)
- emotional goal: the child feeling **safe, loved, and sleepy**

---

## What to avoid

- Excessive repetition of comfort items
- Dominant parent scenes
- Loud or urgent action in the final third
- Emotional clutter or unresolved tension
- Any family member solving the core challenge
