# Adaptive Bedtime Rules

## Core principle

DreamTalez stories are always ~8–10 minutes long. What adapts is the emotional weight, the pacing distribution, and the sensory intensity — never the total runtime. A child's bedtime ritual is sacred.

---

## What adapts and what does not

| Dimension                | Adapts | How |
|--------------------------|--------|-----|
| Story length             | Never  | Always 1200–1500 words |
| Arc structure            | Never  | Opening → Adventure → Middle → Emotional → Ending |
| Emotional arc direction  | Never  | Always decelerate to calm |
| Section word proportions | Yes    | Later hour = longer ending, shorter adventure |
| Prose cadence            | Yes    | Later hour = shorter sentences, slower rhythm |
| Vocabulary complexity    | Yes    | By age profile |
| Sensory intensity        | Yes    | Later hour = fewer, softer cues |
| Comfort anchor emphasis  | Yes    | Recovery mode = more comfort, less novelty |
| Adventure excitement     | Yes    | Suppressed in recovery/overtired states |

---

## Bedtime hour guide

| Hour window | Profile      | Key adjustment |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| 18:00–19:00 | Alert        | Standard arc — give the adventure room |
| 19:00–20:00 | Relaxing     | Begin deceleration at midpoint |
| 20:00–21:00 | Settling     | Shorten sentences from emotional peak onward |
| 21:00–22:00 | Sleepy       | Longer ending, minimal new information |
| 22:00+      | Very sleepy  | Near-lullaby mode — brevity, warmth, pure calm |

---

## Age intelligence guide

| Age range | Register        | Key adjustment |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 2–4       | Very simple     | ≤8 words/sentence, no complexity, repetition welcome |
| 4–6       | Simple          | 8–12 words/sentence, light arc, wonder-forward |
| 6–8       | Moderate        | 10–15 words/sentence, emotional depth OK, one metaphor |
| 8–10      | Rich            | 12–18 words/sentence, theme, layered sensory |
| 10–12     | Advanced        | 15–20 words/sentence, full arc, symbolic magic |

---

## Recovery mode rules

When the previous story was emotionally intense (intensity ≥ 3):

1. Open in warmth — no immediate adventure
2. Suppress antagonists and unresolved tension
3. Comfort anchors must appear before the midpoint and in the ending
4. The ending is proportionally longer than standard
5. At intensity 5 (very high): story is effectively a prose lullaby

---

## Length enforcement

**1200 words minimum. 1500 words maximum. No exceptions.**

Rationale: Children calibrate sleep onset to ritual length. A story that ends too soon disrupts this signal. A story that runs too long triggers fatigue-resistance. Consistent length is the bedtime contract.

---

## What these rules are not

These are pacing and emotional calibration rules, not creative constraints. The child's adventure, the magic, the wonder — these remain fully intact. Adaptive intelligence shapes *how* the story moves through its world, not *what* the story is.
