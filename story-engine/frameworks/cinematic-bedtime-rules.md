# Cinematic Bedtime Rules

## Core rendering principle

Every sentence is either pulling the child deeper into the world, or gently guiding them toward sleep. There is no neutral ground.

---

## Prose rendering standard

Write with:

- **Emotional smoothness** — no jarring jumps in tone or energy
- **Cinematic transitions** — every scene change carries an atmospheric thread from the previous moment
- **Bedtime softness** — pacing decelerates across the story arc; the ending is the quietest section
- **Gentle cadence** — sentences have rhythm; some are long and flowing, some are short and final
- **Sensory timing** — sensory details land at emotional moments, not throughout
- **Emotional breathing room** — at least one quiet decompression moment after the emotional peak

---

## Emotional rhythm arc (follow strictly)

| Story section    | Energy target                            |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------|
| Opening          | Warm + curious — invite gently in        |
| Adventure start  | Wonder-forward — world expands softly    |
| Middle           | Gentle excitement — momentum, never loud |
| Emotional moment | Soft + comforting — slow down, ground    |
| Ending           | Calm + sleepy — almost silence           |

Do not flatten this arc. Do not hold constant energy throughout.

---

## Scene transition rule

Never: "Then [child] went to [place]."

Always: carry one atmospheric element from the current scene — a light, a sound, a sensation — then arrive at the next.

---

## Sensory timing rule

Reserve strong sensory detail for:
1. The moment of greatest wonder
2. The emotional turning point
3. The final calming beat

One well-placed sensory cue creates more immersion than five scattered ones.

---

## Breathing space rule

After the emotional peak, before the ending begins: insert one quiet moment.
Two or three short sentences. No action. No dialogue. Just the world settling.

---

## Ending rules

1. No new information in the final scene
2. Return the child to safety and warmth
3. One physical comfort element (blanket, plushie, warmth)
4. The night world settles — stars, rain, moonlight
5. Final sentence: the shortest, softest, most complete sentence in the story
6. Never "The End." Never a cliffhanger. Never rushed.

---

## What to avoid

- Abrupt transitions ("then they went to…")
- Constant stimulation — no scene should feel like every other
- Noisy pacing — exclamations, sudden events, loud sound words in the second half
- Emotional clutter — unresolved tension at the end
- Sensory spam — sensory detail in every paragraph numbs rather than immerses
- Repetitive comfort phrases — "softly", "quietly", "glowing" used more than three times each
