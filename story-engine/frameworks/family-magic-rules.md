# Family Magic Rules

## Core principle

The child is ALWAYS the hero.

Family members exist to make the child feel loved and capable — never to replace them as the protagonist.

---

## What family members DO

- Appear warmly in the story world
- Provide emotional encouragement at key moments
- Create a sense of safety and belonging
- Name-drop comfort items the child recognises
- Anchor the story in the child's real emotional world

---

## What family members MUST NOT do

- Solve the central problem or challenge
- Dominate scenes or take over narrative focus
- Overshadow the child's bravery, curiosity, or kindness
- Become primary protagonists
- Rescue the child from meaningful story stakes

---

## Comfort continuity

Use the child's comfort items (blanket, plushie, lantern, etc.) as recurring motifs:
- Mentioned naturally in the opening
- Present at the emotional peak as a grounding element
- Referenced softly in the closing

---

## Tone rules

- Maintain bedtime softness throughout
- Keep emotional warmth high and tension low
- Pacing should slow gently across the story arc
- The final paragraph must feel like a loving exhale

---

## Family presence formula

Light touch: one warm appearance per family member maximum.
The child leaves home with their love. They do not bring their family on the adventure — they carry their warmth inside.
